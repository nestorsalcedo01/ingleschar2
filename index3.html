Grammar Gym: Building Stronger Sentences

Grammar isn't just rules; it's the structure of the language. Let's visualize the key concepts from the chapter to make them stick.

Tense Timeline: Past vs. Perfect

P

Past Perfect

Action before a past event.

I had read the book.

S

Past Simple

Action at a specific past time.

I saw the film yesterday.

N

Now (Present)

The present moment.

The Past Perfect happens before the Past Simple. Example: "I had read the book before I saw the film."

Habits vs. Familiarity

Past Habits: `used to` / `would`

To talk about repeated actions or states in the past that are no longer true.

  • `used to`: For habits AND states.
    e.g., I used to live in a small town.
  • `would`: ONLY for habits/actions.
    e.g., We would play outside all day.

Familiarity: `be/get used to`

This is not about the past! It's about adapting to something new.

`get used to` + -ing: The process of adapting.
I am getting used to waking up early.

`be used to` + -ing: The state of being adapted.
I am used to waking up early.

Essay Blueprint

The writing task asks for an opinion essay. Here’s a visual guide to its structure.

Introduction

State the topic and clearly give your opinion (e.g., "I would argue that...").

Body Paragraph 1

Present your first argument with reasons and examples ("For example...").

Body Paragraph 2

Present your second argument, possibly your own idea, with support ("This is why...").

Conclusion

Summarize your points and restate your opinion in a new way ("In conclusion...").

The Art of Conversation

The listening exercises focus on agreeing and disagreeing. This is a key skill for fluent conversation.

Ways to Agree ✅

  • Total Agreement: "Oh, yes, totally!", "Absolutely!", "Me too!", "No doubt about that."
  • Partial Agreement: "You've got a point, but...", "Yes, to some extent, I suppose."

Ways to Disagree ❌

  • Polite Disagreement: "I wouldn't go that far.", "Oh, I wouldn't say that."
  • Strong Disagreement: "You can't be serious!", "Not at all!"
